AFM Cantilever Characterization Publications

During my time at Asylum Research I was involved with several projects was aimed at characterizing the stiffness of the microcantilevers that AFMs use as transducers. For different imaging modes AFMs may resonate the cantilever at very high frequencies, but in order to translate deflection into force data, the effective stiffness at each of the operating resonances must be known. Euler Bernoulli beam approximations can be used for this, but many cantilevers are not rectangular, and some have non uniform cross sections. My contribution to these research projects was in solid modeling, FEA and matlab analysis for stiffness calculation. The work in this project was published in 2 journal articles Please see the linked articles below for more information!

3D Printed AFM Demo Tool: Asylum Research Design Project

This was one of many projects I worked on during my time interning at asylum research. The idea was to create a relatively low-cost product that could be sold to customers or used by the engineering team to demonstrate the principles of different AFM scanning modes. I was the only person on this project, and saw it through from concept generation, to prototyping, to ordering the first small batch of 10 assemblies. The design is built entirely from 3D printed, and off the shelf parts, and accommodates the poor tolerances that come along with 3D printing.
